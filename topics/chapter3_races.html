Species Overview

In the world of Arknights, the land known as Terra is inhabited by various humanoid races. These races are similar to Earth's Homo sapiens, but possess physical traits derived from various animals, legendary creatures, and mythological beings. They can be considered anthropos or anthropomorphic. Unlike most typical fantasy worlds, Terra has virtually no "humans" that fit the Earth definition (although almost all races consider themselves a form of humanity).

Significant differences in physique, physical ability, lifespan, and even Originium Arts adaptability exist between the various humanoid races of Terra. Because these physiological differences are real, racial discrimination on Terra is more severe than on Earth. The Sarkaz, for example, have suffered years of open discrimination and brutal persecution, the depth of which is no less than that against the Infected. Terrans often identify other individuals by race rather than appearance, though cross-race cultural identity groups analogous to Earth's "ethnic nations" do exist (such as Victoria's "Tara" people and Sargon Akahula region's "Tiyekau" people). National borders are also not drawn based on race.

Bestial Terrans (Beastmen): Occasionally, when a Terran humanoid individual experiences atavism or possesses an exceptionally pure bloodline, their prototype animal features almost completely overwhelm their human features, resulting in a nearly fully bestial appearance. Characteristics include a body entirely covered in fur or scales, a beast-like head, digitigrade hind legs, etc. They can essentially be considered beastmen. Interestingly, there seems to be no specific discrimination in Terra against such individuals, nor is there even a specific term for them (meaning beastmen...). Typical examples of beastmen include Spot, Hung, Aak, Wei Yenwu, Yue Mun, the Rat King, "Gopnik," and the native Archosaurians of Sargon.

Cross-Race Hybrids

The races of Terra largely live in mixed communities, so intermarriage is very common. However, generally speaking, children born to parents of different races will inherit the racial traits of only one parent, without any "blending" occurring. It is sometimes possible for siblings to belong to different races, such as the maternal half-sisters Chen and Talulah, who are a Lung and a Draco respectively.

Nevertheless, there are also individuals on Terra who simultaneously inherit traits from multiple races. Such individuals are called Chimeras. Chimeras are typically created by external factors rather than being born with multi-racial traits. These individuals may undergo genetic transformation through surgery, spells, or special powers.


Languages

Your character knows at least three languages: Common, plus two languages you either choose directly from the Standard Languages table or determine by rolling. Knowing a language means your character can communicate in it, read text, and write in it. Your class and other features may also grant you additional languages.
