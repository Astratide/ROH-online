Rules Quick Reference

This page contains quick references from for easy access during gameplay.

Spellcasting

Mastery Damage. All spellcasters in this supplement have their own Mastery Damage, chosen when gaining the Spellcasting feature: Fire, Cold, Acid, Poison, Lightning, or Thunder. Only the Origin Feat "Originium Arts Talent" can grant a caster mastery of Radiant, Necrotic, Force, or Psychic damage. When casting a spell, a caster may convert all damage in the spell to their mastery damage type. However, if they cast a spell that allows choosing from multiple damage types, and one of the options is their mastery damage type, they must choose that type.

Spell List Building. When gaining the Spellcasting feature, all spellcasters in this supplement add their corresponding Basic Spell List and one qualifying Domain Spell List of their choice to their class spell list. This constitutes all available class spells for that caster. Full casters additionally gain their Advanced Spell List at 13th level.

Basic Spell List: Requires no other spellcasting class levels. Contains basic and commonly used spells from levels 0–3. There are three types of Basic Spell Lists: Basic Caster Spells, Basic Medic Spells, and Basic Supporter Spells.

Domain Spell List: Requires the corresponding mastery damage type. Contains spells from levels 0–6 that fit the domain's theme. There are 16 different Domain Spell Lists: Light, Fire, Ice, Water, Air, Earth, Lightning, Darkness, Space, Mind, Illusion, Prophecy, Nature, Poison & Acid, Abyss, and Alchemy.

Advanced Spell List: Requires 13th level and having learned a specific Domain Spell List. Contains the most profound and high-level spells from levels 6–9. There are 7 Advanced Spell Lists: Brilliant Flame, Frigid Torrent, Stormy Thunder, Acid Corrosion, Shadow Soul, Healing Reshaper, and Space-Time Vector.

Preparation. Spellcasters from ROH must prepare spells to cast it even the cantrips. They can only change their prepared list when leveling up.

Upcast Metamagic. When casting a spell at a higher level, regardless of whether the spell has an upcast effect, you may choose to apply a known Metamagic option.

Battleskills

Battleskills. Spend Skill Points (SP), activated by an Action or Bonus Action, sometimes by a Magic action or simultaneously with a successful attack. Most classes allow you to learn one basic Battleskill at 2nd level, and subclasses grant opportunities to learn more at higher levels.

Once Per Turn. You can only use the same Battleskill once per turn.

Skill Points. Your maximum SP equals your Proficiency Bonus. You regain all SP after a Long Rest, and regain SP equal to half your Proficiency Bonus (rounded down) after a Short Rest.

Battleskill Effects. When gaining a new class level, you may apply, replace, or remove a Battleskill Effect on one of your Battleskills from that class. Positive Effects α/β/γ increase SP cost by 1/1.5/2 points respectively; the total SP cost cannot exceed your Proficiency Bonus. Negative Effects reduce SP cost by 1 point (once only).

Recovery. Once per initiative roll, you may use a Bonus Action to recover 1 SP (2 SP from 9th level, 3 SP from 17th level). Each class has its own additional recovery feature:

Vanguard: Choose an ally within 60 feet who can hear you; they recover 1 SP.

Guard: For each SP recovered, gain 1d10 + Constitution modifier temporary HP.

Defender: For each SP recovered, recover 1d12 + Constitution modifier HP.

Sniper: Immediately reload ammunition, or take the Dodge/Disengage action.

Caster: Your next spell is treated as if cast one level higher.

Supporter: Choose an ally within 60 feet who can hear you; they recover 1 SP.

Medic: For each SP recovered, choose an ally within 60 feet who can hear you; they recover 1d6 + spellcasting ability modifier HP.

Specialist: Immediately take the Hide/Dodge/Dash/Disengage action.

Power Strike. You may spend 1 SP when you hit with an attack to activate this Battleskill. The attack immediately deals additional damage equal to a number of d6 equal to your Proficiency Bonus.

Swift Strike. You may spend 1 SP as a Bonus Action to activate this Battleskill. Until the end of this turn, when you take the Attack action, you may make one additional weapon attack.

Tactical Chant. You may spend 1 SP as a Bonus Action to activate this Battleskill. Until the end of this turn, when you take the Magic action, you may cast one cantrip with a casting time of 1 action as part of the same Magic action.

ATK Up. You may spend 1 SP as a Bonus Action to activate this Battleskill. Until the end of this turn, you gain +1d4 on attack rolls and add your Proficiency Bonus to damage rolls.

Conditions

Inspiration. A creature with Inspiration gains +1d4 on attack rolls and damage rolls.

Sanctuary. When a creature with Sanctuary takes damage, for every 10 points of damage taken, they may roll 1d4 to reduce the damage by the total rolled.

Fragile. Whenever you take damage of a single type, for every 10 points of that damage, you take an additional 1d4 damage of the same type (this additional damage does not trigger itself).

Resist. For a creature with Resist, conditions that would end at the end of its turn instead end at the start of its turn. Conditions that allow a repeated save at the end of its turn to end may instead allow the save at the start of its turn. Additionally, it has Advantage on saving throws to avoid or end a condition or hazard.

Debility. A creature suffering from Debility has a −5 penalty on damage rolls.

Shudder. A creature suffering from Shudder cannot make attack rolls. Creatures immune to the Frightened condition are also immune to Shudder.

Silence. A creature suffering from Silence cannot speak, cast spells with verbal components, or use Battleskills. Additionally, if a trait specifies that it can be silenced, the creature cannot benefit from that trait while Silenced.

Oripathy

Oripathy Level. Oripathy levels range from 1 to 8. Unless medication is taken shortly after an infection level increases, the infection level cannot be reduced by any means. Below are the infection types and their common symptoms:

Minimal Infection (Level 1): Occasional fever.

Mild Infection (Level 2–3): Occasional fever, nausea, dizziness; a small amount of surface Originium crystals.

Moderate Infection (Level 4–5): Frequent fever, nausea, dizziness; noticeable surface Originium crystals.

Severe Infection (Level 6–7): Loss of some bodily function or sensation; partial crystallization of organs or body parts, along with the above symptoms.

Lethal Infection (Level 8): Loss of more bodily function or sensation; severe crystallization of organs or multiple body parts, along with the above symptoms.

Oripathy Infection Check. An Oripathy Infection Check is a Constitution saving throw. If you fail this check, your infection level increases by 1. You must make an Oripathy Infection Check in the following two situations:

Taking Originium Damage: Damage is divided into Originium-based and non-Originium-based. A creature that takes Originium damage must make an Oripathy Infection Check (DC equals half the damage taken, minimum 10, maximum 30). A creature only needs to make this check once per round.

Long Rest While Injured: If you have an infection level and your HP is not full before starting a Long Rest, you must make an Oripathy Infection Check upon completing the Long Rest (DC equals 8 + your current infection level).

Acute Oripathy Outburst (N1): When you roll a 1 on the d20 for an Oripathy Infection Check, you trigger an Acute Oripathy Outburst.

When you gain Acute Oripathy Outburst, you immediately gain a number of Exhaustion levels equal to half your infection level (rounded down). You must also roll and expend a number of Hit Dice equal to your current infection level, and lose HP equal to the total rolled.
If you don't have enough Hit Dice, you fall Unconscious. You can end this Unconsciousness by succeeding on a Constitution saving throw once per hour (DC equals 8 + your current infection level).

Active Originium Enhancement (N20): When you roll a 20 on the d20 for an Oripathy Infection Check, you trigger Active Originium Enhancement.

When you gain Active Originium Enhancement, you immediately roll and expend a number of Hit Dice equal to your current infection level, and lose HP equal to the total rolled. For the next 24 hours, you gain a bonus to damage rolls equal to your infection level.

Infected Dissolution. When a creature with an Oripathy infection level dies, their remains dissolve within 1d20 minutes. The remains dissipate into ash and cannot be resurrected by spell effects lower than 9th level. If the creature has Severe Infection or higher, the dissolution instead occurs within 1d6 minutes, and the area within 15 feet of their remains becomes a Moderate Originium Contamination Zone, with the next 15 feet beyond that becoming a Light Originium Contamination Zone.

Originium Contamination Zones. Originium Contamination Zones are divided into 1–5 levels:

Minimal Originium Contamination (Level 1): Commonly found in urban core industrial areas, power districts, Originium mining areas, some wilderness areas, or underground caves.

Light or Moderate Originium Contamination (Level 2–3): Commonly found in artillery-bombarded battlefields, high-yield Originium explosive detonations, after Severe Infected dissolution, Catastrophe aftermath, Originium reactor leaks, etc.

Severe or Lethal Originium Contamination (Level 4–5): Commonly found at the center of Catastrophes, large-scale high-yield Originium explosive detonations, etc.

Increased Susceptibility: A creature in any Originium Contamination Zone has Disadvantage on Oripathy checks.

Active Particle Inhalation: When entering a Level 2–3 Originium Contamination Zone, or for every hour spent within the zone, a creature takes Originium Necrotic damage equal to a number of d6 equal to the zone's contamination level.

Active Particle Erosion: When entering a Level 4–5 or higher Originium Contamination Zone, or for every minute spent within the zone, a creature takes Originium Necrotic damage equal to a number of d6 equal to the zone's contamination level.

Infected Spellcasting. An Infected individual may use the Originium within their body as a spellcasting focus to satisfy any components required to cast a spell, including costly materials. When casting a spell this way, they must make a Constitution saving throw (Oripathy Infection Check) with a DC equal to 8 + the spell's level. On a failure, their infection level increases by 1.

Suppressing Oripathy. You may spend 10 minutes focusing on treatment to help suppress the disease in a nearby Infected individual. When you complete the treatment, make a Medicine skill check (DC = 10 + the target's infection level). Using a charge from a Healer's Kit grants Advantage on this check. Any Oripathy medication grants a +5 bonus on this check. On a success, you can remove one level of Exhaustion caused by Oripathy from the target, or wake them from Unconsciousness. Regardless of success or failure, the target cannot receive this treatment again for 24 hours.
