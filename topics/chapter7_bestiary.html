Bestiary

Enemies are divided into three tiers: Normal, Elite, and Leader. Enemies are the primary challenge players face in the game, and defeating them is a key path to progression and skill improvement.

The appearance of enemies in an encounter often signifies the progression of the story. While obstructing the players' goals, they also pursue objectives of their own. Proper and well-rounded enemy design—including motives, personality, faction affiliation, race, and backstory—can add depth and layers to your game world.

Furthermore, the threat and presence of enemies are important factors in building narrative tension. Their appearance often foreshadows an intense battle or a major plot development, capturing players' attention and enhancing the game's immersion and appeal. Certain powerful Leader or Elite enemies, especially key antagonists in the story, have their own independent backstories and motives, revealing the complex relationships and conflicts behind the setting.


Creature Types

All creatures in D&D, including all player characters, have a specific keyword in the rules that identifies their creature type. The vast majority of player characters are Humanoids. The race options listed here indicate the creature tags a monster might possess.

Humanoid (Terran). The vast majority of Terran races are Humanoids, including Sarkaz, Sankta, Perro, Feline, Aegir, and others. They consider themselves a form of humanity, even though they possess many diverse animal and fantasy creature traits.

Celestial / Dragon (Feranmut). Feranmuts are nearly immortal, quasi-deific beings that wield powers entirely different from Originium Arts. They are often widely worshipped by the Terran peoples in their vicinity. Some Feranmuts can generate independent ecosystems within their bodies, and some are themselves part of the environment. Feranmuts may sometimes appear in human society through avatars, or rely on proxies to convey their will.

Construct (Drone). Drones are usually remotely controlled or autonomous inorganic combat aerial vehicles, generally possessing a flying speed.

Construct (Mechanic). Mechanics are usually inorganic vehicles or mechs, typically operated by a living being from within.

Undead (Possessed). The Possessed are a collective term for mutated Infected who have been deeply parasitized and enhanced by a special form of Originium. This influence transforms them into zombie-like feral creatures, lacking independent personality or emotion but endowed with enhanced physique, resilience, explosive strength, and numbness to pain. Mephisto's "Herd" and the "Forsaken" of the Rim Billiton mines are both types of Possessed creatures.

Aberration (Seaborn). Seaborn are a collective term for strange enemies from the sea, also known as sea monsters. These monsters are generally commanded by a special hive mind called the "Great Will" and possess extraordinary evolutionary speed and aggression.

Elemental / Fey / Construct / Aberration (Arts Creature). Arts Creatures are a collective term for special beings artificially created by Originium Arts, such as the Mudrock Colossi and Tombstone Guardians created by Gargoyles, or the Filth Eaters created by Nachzehrers. Additionally, summons conjured by player spells typically possess the Arts Creature tag.

Elemental / Fey / Construct / Aberration (Apparition). Apparitions are a collective term for special derivatives born from the "Sui" Feranmut. This includes Ink Spirits, Vessel Haunts, Fabric Haunts, and other special derivatives, generally only appearing in Yan.

Aberration (Collapsal). Collapsals are a collective term for malevolent extradimensional entities. They possess characteristics such as formlessness, indescribability, cognitive damage, and assimilative influence. They are the common nemesis of all Terran civilizations and are primarily found in the far north. Crazelyseon and Woodsplitter Warriors are typical Collapsal creatures.
